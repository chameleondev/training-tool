Potential outcomes of stem cell stimulation by eltrombopag
  • Acquired aplastic anaemia is thought to occur after bone marrow injury (e.g. by viral infection)
    • This results in immune-mediated damage to HSCs and an abnormal inflammatory immune response characterised by:
      • Expansion of the cytotoxic CD8 T-cell and CD4 T-helper cell populations
      • Abnormalities in regulatory T-cell function
  • Stem cells may be stimulated with drug treatments:
    • Immunosuppressive therapy (IST) with horse or rabbit ATG in combination with CsA or Campath OR
    • Thrombopoietin receptor agonists (such as eltrombopag)
  • One of the following outcomes has been proposed to occur after stem cell stimulation:
    • No recovery because there are too few HSCs to respond to either IST or eltrombopag
    • Near complete peripheral blood recovery, but the bone marrow remains hypocellular
    • Complete recovery of blood and bone marrow
    • Recovery with subsequent development of cytogenetic abnormalities, proliferation of an abnormal stem cell population and possible development of disorders such as MDS and paroxysmal nocturnal haemoglobinuria
