Phase II study in refractory aplastic anaemia:
data analysis
  • To test the null hypothesis, response probability was set at ≤10%1
    • The null hypothesis would be accepted if the number of patients with a response at 12 weeks was five or less
  • Response probability for the alternative hypothesis was set at ≥30%1
  • The initial phase of the trial included 25 patients, based on testing of the null hypothesis at a significance level of 0.05 and a power of 80%1
  • Additional patients were enrolled in an extension study, to focus on duration and quality of response, the impact of eltrombopag discontinuation in robust responders and the incidence of cytogenetic abnormalities2
    • A further 18 patients were enrolled in the expanded trial to give a total of 44 patients with refractory SAA
      • 43 patients received eltrombopag treatment

Phase II study in refractory aplastic anaemia: baseline characteristics (cohorts 1 and 2)
  • The combination of the key endpoints of the phase II study and the baseline characteristics of the patient population were the basis of the dose-adjustment criteria used in the prescribing information for eltrombopag
    • In multilineage responders, once platelet count is >50×109/l, haemoglobin is >10 g/dl in the absence of red blood cell (RBC) transfusions and absolute neutrophil count (ANC) is >1×109/l for more than 8 weeks, the dose of eltrombopag should be reduced by up to 50%2
    • If these counts stay stable after 8 weeks at the reduced dose, eltrombopag may be discontinued2
      • This is in agreement with the duration of transfusion independence and the main efficacy criteria specified in the phase II study1
