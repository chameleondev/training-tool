White blood cell subtypes and their functions
  • One of the largest organs in the body
    • Volume of ~5 litres
  • Classed as a connective tissue
  • Composed of three main cell types suspended in plasma
    • Erythrocytes (red blood cells; RBCs)
    • Leukocytes (white blood cells; WBCs)
    • Thrombocytes (platelets)
The blood contains erythrocytes, leukocytes and
thrombocytes suspended in plasma.
Each cell type supports tissue function
