Erythrocytes (red blood cells)
  • Red blood cells (RBCs) are the most abundant of all blood cell types
    • ~5×1012 cells/l
    • Biconcave discs ~8.4 μm in diameter
  • Immature RBCs: reticulocytes
    • Develop in the red marrow before entering the circulation and maturing to RBCs
  • Mature RBCs: erythrocytes
    • Contain a high concentration of haemoglobin (Hb), the O2-carrying pigment
    • No nucleus present
  • Primary function: gas exchange
    • O2: from lungs to tissues
    • CO2: from tissues to lungs
  • Circulatory lifespan of ~120 days
RBCs are identified by their characteristic
biconcave discoid shape
