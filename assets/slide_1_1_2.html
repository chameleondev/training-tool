Erythrocytes (red blood cells)
  • Red blood cells (RBCs) are the most abundant of all blood cell types
    • ~5×1012 cells/l
    • Biconcave discs ~8.4 μm in diameter
  • Immature RBCs: reticulocytes
    • Develop in the red marrow before entering the circulation and maturing to RBCs
  • Mature RBCs: erythrocytes
    • Contain a high concentration of haemoglobin (Hb), the O2-carrying pigment
    • No nucleus present
  • Primary function: gas exchange
    • O2: from lungs to tissues
    • CO2: from tissues to lungs
  • Circulatory lifespan of ~120 days
RBCs are identified by their characteristic
biconcave discoid shape
Erythrocytes (red blood cells)

Mature erythrocytes constitute ~45% of blood by volume

  • Benefits of the biconcave shape of the cell:
    • Flexibility, allowing travel through narrow capillaries
    • Maximised surface area: volume ratio for efficient gas exchange across the membrane
  • Haemoglobin contains two components:
    • Haem (iron-containing porphyrin)
    • Globin (protein)
  • Mature circulating erythrocytes have no nucleus, ribosomes or mitochondria
    • They derive energy from the anaerobic glycolytic pathway
  • Circulatory lifespan is ~120 days, before being sequestered in the spleen and consumed by the phagocytic cells of the reticuloendothelial system
