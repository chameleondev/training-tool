Haematopoiesis in adults: overview
  • Haematopoietic stem cells (HSCs) differentiate into two stem cell types:
    • Myeloid
    • Lymphoid
  • These precursors give rise to different blood cell types
  • The different processes that give rise to the different blood cell types are as follows:
    • Erythropoiesis: RBCs
    • Myelopoiesis: granulocytes and monocytes
      • Granulopoiesis: granulocytes
      • Monopoiesis: monocytes
  • Thrombopoiesis: platelets
  • Lymphopoiesis: WBCs
