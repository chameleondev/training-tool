Global distribution of aplastic anaemia
  • Incidence in Western countries is approximately two cases per 1 million people/year1,2
  • In East Asia, the incidence is 2–3 times higher than in Western countries3
    • Range: 5–7 per 1 million people
    • In rural Thailand, exposure to non-bottled water, certain animals, animal fertiliser, pesticides and non-medical needles suggests an infectious aetiology
  • The rarity of aplastic anaemia may be accounted for by a combination of:4
    • Infrequent exposure events
    • Diversity in genetic predisposing factors
    • Individual variation in immune response
