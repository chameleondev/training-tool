Classification of aplastic anaemia/bone marrow failure syndromes
  • Aplastic anaemia/bone marrow failure syndromes are a diverse group of disorders characterised by the inability of the bone marrow to produce healthy levels of blood cells
  • ~15–20% of cases: aplastic anaemia is the result of an inherited genetic disorder1 (e.g. Fanconi anaemia [the most common type], dyskeratosis congenita, Pearson syndrome, Shwachman-Diamond syndrome or Familial aplastic anaemia)
  • ~70% of acquired aplastic anaemia cases: idiopathic because the primary cause has not been elucidated
  • 10–15% of cases: secondary to an identifiable factor
    • Causes may include chronic exposure to therapeutic radiation, chemotherapeutic agents, idiosyncratic responses to drugs such as chloramphenicol, or immune-related (such as systemic lupus erythrematosus)
