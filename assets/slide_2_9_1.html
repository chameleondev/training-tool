Quality-adjusted survival analysis: bone marrow transplant versus immunosuppressive therapy
  • A retrospective, single-centre study was conducted to compare survival and quality of life outcomes (from 1976 to 1999) between aplastic anaemia patients treated with BMT and those treated with IST
  • Survival, event-free survival and Q-TWIST (quality adjusted time without symptoms or toxicity) for both groups were compared
  • Overall responses or event-free survival rates were similar for both therapies
    • However, treatment-related deaths were significantly higher with BMT compared with IST
  • BMT-treated patients experienced a shorter duration of treatment-related toxicity and longer time in complete remission without medication compared with IST-treated patients; however, this was not statistically significant
    • IST-treated patients require closer medical care, transfusion support and medications
  • Published literature on the cost aspects of severe aplastic anaemia is limited, especially in adults
