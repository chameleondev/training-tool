Summary
  • Aplastic anaemia is characterised by haematopoietic failure and multilineage cytopenia
  • Eltrombopag may stimulate haematopoiesis by non-competitive activation of TPO-R
    • The ability of TPO to act on progenitor stem cells supports a hypothetical role for TPO-R agonists in treating bone marrow failure
  • In this first phase II clinical study of eltrombopag for the treatment of IST-refractory SAA, treatment was associated with:
    • Improved blood counts in â‰¥1 cell line compared with baseline, with a multilineage response observed over time
    • Multilineage responses maintained over time (median duration of response >1 year) and trilineage responses maintained after discontinuation of eltrombopag
      • Associated with restoration of bone marrow cellularity and platelet and RBC transfusion-independent intervals of >6 months in a population that was transfusion dependent
  • Eltrombopag at doses up to 150 mg daily was generally well tolerated in the phase II study
    • Overall safety profile was consistent with the expected safety profile for patients with SAA and the established safety profile of eltrombopag in approved indications
    • No new safety signals were detected
    • However, an increased incidence of cytogenetic abnormalities, which are a known potential outcome for SAA, was noted with eltrombopag treatment
      • The clinical consequences of these changes require further study and, if observed during treatment with eltrombopag, it is recommended that treatment with eltrombopag be discontinued
  • Eltrombopag (PROMACTA/REVOLADE) is a breakthrough therapy that may change the treatment paradigm for SAA, by introducing a novel approach in the management of the disease
