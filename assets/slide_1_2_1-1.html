The bone marrow is the site of haematopoiesis in adults
  • Bone marrow: composed of both haemopoietic and non-haemopoietic cells anchored within a structural network known as the extracellular matrix (ECM)1
  • Many blood cells are short-lived and need to be replenished continuously2
  • Continued production of these cells depends on the presence of haematopoietic stem cells (HSCs)2
    • HSCs: progenitors that differentiate into the various types of mature blood cells, including red blood cells (RBCs) and lymphocytes2
