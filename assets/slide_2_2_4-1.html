Acquired aplastic anaemia: general aetiology
  • Acquired (idiopathic) aplastic anaemia is thought to arise from T-cell-mediated autoimmune attack of HSCs1
    • The responsiveness of aplastic anaemia to immunosuppression provides the strongest support for an autoimmune pathophysiology1
  • Exposure to certain chemical agents and drugs has been suggested as a cause2
    • However, neither are able to account for a significant proportion of acquired aplastic anaemia cases1
  • Aplastic anaemia may also occur as a rare complication:1
    • After pregnancy
    • As part of immunological disease (e.g. eosinophilic fasciitis)
  • Aplastic anaemia can also follow an episode of seronegative hepatitis (A, B or C)1,3
    • This constitutes ~5–10% of all cases of aplastic anaemia1
    • Most often affects males, with the onset of aplastic anaemia occurring 2–3 months after viral infection3
