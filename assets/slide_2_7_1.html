Outcomes in severe aplastic anaemia:
evolution over time
  • Overall survival rates with bone marrow transplantation (BMT) have improved over time
  • The success of BMT is strongly correlated with age and donor type
  • Studies suggest that 5-year survival after matched donor transplantation may be as follows:1
    • 80–90% in patients aged <20 years
    • 50–70% in patients aged 20–40 years
    • ~50% in patients aged >40 years
  • After unrelated donor (UD) transplantation, survival rates are more variable and may not be as high as those observed after matched donor transplantation (28–94%)2
  • However, graft-versus-host disease and the risk of transplant rejection still remain major concerns for severe aplastic anaemia patients3,4
  • Despite improvements in antimicrobial support therapy, 5-year survival rates among patients refractory to IST is still <60%, indicating the need for new, effective treatments for patients who may not be eligible for transplant but are no longer responsive to IST5
