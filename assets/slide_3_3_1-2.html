Phase II study in refractory aplastic anaemia: key endpoints
Laboratory tests
Haematological parameter Definition
Platelets Increase of ≥20,000 cells/mm3 from baseline

OR

Transfusion independence for ≥8 weeks in patients who were previously transfusion-dependent
Erythrocytes Increase in haemoglobin level (from a pre-treatment baseline of <9 g/dl) by ≥1.5 g/dl without packed RBC transfusion

OR

Reduced number of packed RBC units transfused by ≥4 units for 8 consecutive weeks, compared with requirements during the 8 weeks before study entry
Neutrophils Increase of ≥100% in absolute neutrophil count (ANC) in patients with a baseline value of <500 cells/mm3 , or an ANC increase of >500 cells/mm3 in patients with pre-treatment ANC of ≥500 cells/mm3
Phase II study in refractory aplastic anaemia: key endpoints
  • Secondary endpoints included:
    • Changes in blood counts measured as continuous variables
    • Bone marrow parameters: cellularity, haematopoiesis and morphological characteristics
    • Metaphase cytogenetic profile
    • Toxicity, as measured with the National Cancer Institute Common Terminology Criteria for Adverse Events Version 3
