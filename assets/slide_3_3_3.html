Phase II study: eltrombopag produces a multilineage response over time in refractory aplastic anaemia
  • Of the 17 patients from cohort 1 who achieved the primary endpoint, 14 entered the extension arm
    • Of these, six had improvement in more than 1 cell lineage after continued eltrombopag treatment
      • Three patients with a unilineage response improved to a bilineage response
      • One patient with a unilineage response achieved a trilineage response
      • Two patients with a bilineage response improved to a trilineage response
  • In 14 patients, the last response assessed was the same as the best response

Phase II study in refractory aplastic anaemia: normalisation of bone marrow cellularity
  • At baseline, 15 of the 17 patients who responded to eltrombopag had hypocellular bone marrow ranging from ‘variable’ to ‘severe’2
  • During treatment with eltrombopag, normalisation of bone marrow cellularity occurred in five responders2
    • Median duration of treatment (649 days [range 274–812])
  • Seven additional responders demonstrated improved bone marrow cellularity from baseline2
  • No difference from baseline assessment was noted in five patients2
