Treatment of relapsed severe aplastic anaemia
  • There is no consensus on the definition of relapse in severe aplastic anaemia1
    • Reintroduction of IST is required for decreasing blood counts, with or without restarting blood product transfusions1
  • In relapsed cases, response rates to r-ATG/CsA are 68% at 6 months2
    • Reintroduction or an increase in CsA dose for 2–3 months is a simple way of treating relapse1
  • Treatment may be continued until blood counts improve (at least 6–12 months), after which the CsA dose may be tapered off1
    • However, a gradual decline in blood counts may occur with the taper3
    • The aim is to use the lowest possible dose of CsA to maintain adequate blood counts3
      • However, the patient may become increasingly intolerant to treatment as blood counts continue to deteriorate3
  • When a haematological response is not achieved with IST and severe pancytopenia persists, transplant options must be pursued1
