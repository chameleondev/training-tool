3.1
Eltrombopag: mode of action
Learning Objectives
  • By the end of this module you should be able to:
    • Understand the details of the clinical trials and the outcomes relating to eltrombopag use in severe aplastic anaemia (SAA)
      • Preclinical data in mice show that eltrombopag can stimulate multilineage haematopoiesis
      • Phase II results demonstrate that eltrombopag can improve haematopoiesis in â‰¥1 cell lineage in patients with refractory aplastic anaemia
        • A multilineage haematological response was observed in some patients, with an increase in platelet, red blood cell (RBC) and neutrophil levels after treatment with eltrombopag
        • The number of cell lineages that met haematological response improved over time for some patients
        • Response was maintained in patients whose treatment was tapered because of improvements in defined multilineage blood counts (platelets, erythrocytes and neutrophils)
    • Appreciate that cytogenetic abnormalities and subsequent evolution to haematological malignancies are known long-term risks in patients with bone marrow disorders, particularly in patients with SAA
      • Patients with aplastic anaemia are at risk for developing myelodysplastic syndrome (MDS) and acute myeloid leukaemia (AML)
