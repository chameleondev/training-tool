Adaptive immunity: humoral response
  • Requires prior exposure to the antigen1,2
  • Foreign antigen is recognised by circulating antibodies or cell surface receptors on B-cells1,2
  • Receptor–antigen complexes are taken into the B-cell, triggering the production of clones1
    • Most of these clones become plasma cells that secrete antigen-specific antibodies
    • The remaining clones become memory cells
  • The antigen–antibody complex/complement-microorganism complex is phagocytised2
The humoral response involves the transformation
of B-cells to antibody-producing plasma cells1,2
