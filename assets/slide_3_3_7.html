Phase II study in refractory aplastic anaemia:
maintained response after eltrombopag discontinuation
  • Heavily pre-treated patients, for example patients enrolled in this study, are at a high risk of infectious and bleeding complications because of pancytopenia1
    • Approximately 25% of IST-refractory patients with SAA die from bleeding or infection
  • A total of 14 patients experienced at least one serious AE during treatment2
    • The most common serious AEs reported were febrile neutropenia, sepsis and viral infection
    • One patient experienced severe abdominal discomfort that was considered to be treatment-related by the investigators
  • None of the six deaths (14%) reported during the study were considered to be related to eltrombopag
    • The primary cause of death was sepsis/infection due to disease (four patients)
Phase II study in refractory aplastic anaemia: serious adverse events and death
  • No deaths occurred during eltrombopag treatment:2
    • Two patients died of sepsis/infection within 30 days of the last dose of eltrombopag
    • Four subjects died more than 110 days after the last dose of eltrombopag
    • Three patients, whose primary cause of death was reported as disease under study, had fatal serious AEs of aplastic anaemia, sepsis and septic shock
