Rationale for eltrombopag use in aplastic anaemia
  • Eltrombopag interacts with the transmembrane domain of the human TPO-R and initiates signalling cascades that induce proliferation and differentiation of megakaryocytes from bone marrow progenitor cells1,2
    • Effects may occur despite elevated levels of TPO3
    • The different binding sites of eltrombopag and TPO on the TPO-R prevents competitive binding and potentially allows both molecules to have an additive effect on cell signalling4
    • Eltrombopag activity is dependent on TPO-R expression because no activity has been shown in cells expressing receptors for other haematopoietic growth factors, including EPO and granulocyte colony-stimulating factor (G-CSF)4
  • A lack of response to IST may be due to the limited number of haematopoietic stem cells5
    • Treatment with a combination of IST with growth factors (e.g. G-CSF and EPO) was proposed to overcome the limited response to IST alone, but this approach is of limited use6
    • This may be due to growth factors acting on more committed stem cells, rather than on early-stage progenitors7
  • The ability of TPO to act on less-differentiated progenitor cells suggests that TPO-R agonists may be effective in the treatment of diseases associated with bone marrow failure, such as SAA7
Rationale for eltrombopag use in aplastic anaemia
  • Studies showed that eltrombopag stimulated megakaryopoiesis in bone marrow cells from patients with myelodysplastic syndrome (MDS),8 a condition in which TPO levels are elevated, although to a lesser extent than in patients with aplastic anaemia9
  • Clinical trials of haematopoietic growth factors such as granulocyte colony-stimulating factor (G-CSF), granulocyte-macrophage colony-stimulating factor (GM-CSF), stem cell factor and interleukin-3 have not demonstrated efficacy in aplastic anaemia7
    • Cytokine receptors are not present on primitive stem cells
    • Endogenous erythropoietin and G-CSF levels are highly elevated in SAA
  • Preclinical evidence supports a potential role for TPO-R agonists in the treatment of cytopenias arising from bone marrow failure
    • Eltrombopag accelerated the expansion of HSCs/HPCs in human umbilical cord blood cells in an in vivo mouse model10
    • Multilineage haematopoiesis was also observed with eltrombopag10
      • Circulating levels of human platelets and human white blood cells (WBCs) were increased

  • An effect of eltrombopag on both human umbilical cord blood HSC expansion and haematopoiesis has been observed in preclinical studies
    • Circulating levels of human platelets and WBCs were increased in a non-obese diabetic/severe combined immunodeficiency (NOD/SCID) xenotransplant mouse model in vivo
      • Expansion of human stem cells and progenitors in the bone marrow was enhanced with eltrombopag
    • Eltrombopag enhanced expansionof HSCs and megakaryocytes in vitro

Effect of eltrombopag on expansion of haematopoietic stem/progenitor cells and multilineage haematopoiesis
  • The effect of eltrombopag on haematopoiesis of human umbilical cord blood stem cells and progenitor cells (CD34+) transplanted into immunodeficient mice was investigated
    • Eltrombopag increased circulating levels of human platelets and WBCs compared with control, but had no effect on murine thrombopoiesis or WBC counts
    • Analysis of human cell surface markers on cells extracted from bone marrow revealed that HSCs/HPCs (CD34+), WBCs (CD45+) and cells from the thrombocyte lineage (CD41+) were all increased with eltrombopag versus control
      • Proliferation of mouse bone marrow cells was not affected by treatment with eltrombopag
