Leukocytes (white blood cells)
  • White blood cells (WBCs) are the least abundant of all blood cell types
    • ~5×109 cells/l
    • Contain a nucleus
    • Can migrate out of the blood into specific tissues
  • Granulocytes (make up 50–75% of total leukocytes)
    • Involved in the rapid, generalised immune response
    • Neutrophils, eosinophils and basophils
  • Mononuclear leukocytes (make up 20–50% of total leukocytes)
    • Involved in a slower immune response compared with granulocytes
    • Monocytes (non-specific phagocytic cells) and lymphocytes (antigen-specific immune cells)
