Summary
  • The blood is composed of three main cell types: erythrocytes, leukocytes and thrombocytes
  • In adults, the continued production of these cells depends on the presence of HSCs in the bone marrow
  • Growth and differentiation of the different types of blood cells is governed by a complex interplay of cytokines, including EPO, TPO and G-CSF
  • The innate and adaptive arms of the immune system interact to recognise “self” and eliminate foreign antigens
  • Autoimmune conditions, including aplastic anaemia, are thought to arise from an inability of the immune system to recognise the body’s own tissues
