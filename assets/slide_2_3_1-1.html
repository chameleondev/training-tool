Pathophysiology of acquired aplastic anaemia: overview
  • HSCs are affected by ligand–receptor interactions, intracellular signalling and gene cell activation
  • (A) Activated cytotoxic T lymphocytes secrete lymphokines such as interferon gamma (IFN-γ) and tumour necrosis factor (TNF), both of which can upregulate the expression of each other’s cellular receptors, as well as the Fas receptor
  • (B) Increased interleukin-2 (IL-2) production leads to polyclonal expansion of the T-cell population
  • (C) Activation of the Fas receptor by the Fas ligand present on lymphocytes leads to apoptosis of target cells
  • (D) IFN-γ may influence the ability of the cell to enter the cell cycle through interferon regulatory factor 1 (IRF-1)
    • It is also a potent inducer of cellular genes involved in cell death, including nitric oxide synthase (NOS), which produces the toxic gas, nitric oxide (NO)
