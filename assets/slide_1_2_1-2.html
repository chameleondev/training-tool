Bone marrow stroma
  • The bone marrow stroma provides the microenvironment and growth factors needed to facilitate haematopoeisis,1,2 including:
    • Interleukins (ILs)3
    • Colony-stimulating factors (CSFs)3
    • Erythropoietin (EPO)3
    • Thrombopoietin (TPO)3
  • Thrombopoietin was originally thought to be specific to platelet maturation from MK cells, but has been shown to promote survival of all haematopoietic stem cells (HSCs)4,5
    • All HSCs express TPO receptors (TPO-Rs; also known as MpL)6
Bone marrow stroma
  • Stromal cell types include:
    • Fibroblasts, endothelial cells and macrophages1
    • Progenitors of skeletal tissue components (e.g. bone, cartilage, haematopoiesis-supporting stroma and adipocytes)2,3
    • Many stromal cells are important sources of haemopoietic growth factors1

Thrombopoietin and thrombopoietin receptors
  • Binding of TPO to TPO-Rs on the surface of MK cells triggers the proliferation and maturation cascade that results in mature circulating platelets1
  • However, the expression of TPO-R on all HSCs suggests that TPO has a broader role in HSC survival and haematopoiesis, in addition to platelet generation1,2
  • Cell culture studies have shown that TPO activity may not be lineage-specific2
  • TPO-R deficiencies/defects are associated with haematological diseases
    • In mice: bone marrow aplasia3
    • In humans:
      • Multi-lineage marrow failure associated with congenital amegakaryocytic thrombocytopenia4
      • Familial aplastic anaemia5
Thrombopoietin and thrombopoietin receptors
  • Cell culture studies have shown that TPO activity may not be lineage-specific1
    • In suspended cell culture: TPO promotes HSC survival but not proliferation1
    • Culture medium + IL-3/stem cell factor (SCF): accelerates HSC entry into the cell cycle, resulting in a high output of colony-forming cells of all lineages1,2
    • Receptor expression studies: in addition to MK cells, TPO-Rs (also known as MpL) may be expressed on a variety of cells derived from the bone marrow3,4 (e.g. CD34+ cells1,4)

The thrombopoietin receptor is expressed at all stages of stem cell differentiation
TPO-R is expressed in cells from the uncommitted progenitor stem cell stage to specific cell lineages, including megakaryocytes
Thrombopoietin receptor expression decreases as haematopoietic stem cells differentiation progresses
  • CD34 and CD38 antigen are useful in identifying the stages of cell differentiation and commitment to a specific lineage
  • The four stages of differentiation are:
    • Stage I (CD34++/CD38-) and stage II (CD34++/CD38+/++): uncommitted multipotent progenitor cells
    • Stage III (CD34+/CD38+++) and stage IV (CD34diminished/CD38+++): lineage committed progenitor cells
  • The expression of TPO (c-MpL) is significantly higher at stage I and decreases with advancing differentiation stage
