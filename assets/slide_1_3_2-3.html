Leukocytes (white blood cells)
  • The formation of platelets starts with the development of a megakaryoblast from CFUs (precursor cells) by endoreduplication to become MK cells1
  • Through successive endomitotic multiplication of the nuclear DNA content, the MKs transform into a giant polyploid cell, the pro-megakaryocyte, and finally into the platelet-forming MK cells1
  • The MK cytoplasm is then packaged into multiple long projections known as proplatelets which are released into vascular sinusoids
    • Once in the bloodstream, proplatelets lose their nuclei and mature into platelets2

Thrombopoiesis
Thrombopoiesis
  • MK cells:
    • Have increased cell size and are several times the normal diploid content of DNA1
    • The MK cytoplasm matures and forms projections known as proplatelets, which are released from the bone marrow into the circulation, where they mature into platelets2
    • Proliferation and maturation are stimulated by TPO1
    • These cells are synthesized in various tissues including in the liver, kidney and marrow stroma1,3
