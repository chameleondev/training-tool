Role of thrombopoietin in aplastic anaemia
  • Aplastic anaemia is a disorder characterised by pancytopenia (multilineage bone marrow failure) and a very limited number of HSCs/haematopoietic progenitor cells (HPCs) due to an immune-mediated attack on the bone marrow1
    • Levels of the most primitive cells remain persistently and severely low, even with immunosuppressive therapy (IST)1
    • Thrombocytopenia has been reported in most patients with c-mpl mutations2
    • Serum TPO levels are markedly elevated in all patients with aplastic anaemia3,4
  • Several preclinical experiments have shown the positive effect of TPO and TPO-R on the expansion of HSCs/HPCs5–8

Role of thrombopoietin in aplastic anaemia
  • Serum TPO levels are markedly elevated in all patients with aplastic anaemia3,4
    • Median TPO levels are 22-fold higher than in normal controls3
    • No correlation was observed between TPO levels and platelet counts3
      • However, higher levels of TPO were observed in patients requiring platelet transfusion compared with those who had higher platelet counts (thus platelet transfusions were not required)
    • After synthesis by the liver, TPO levels are passively regulated by TPO binding to TPO-Rs on platelets and megakaryocytes9
