Pathophysiology of acquired aplastic anaemia: T-cell-mediated destruction of bone marrow
  • An immune response, led by an expanded cytotoxic T-cell population, targets HSCs and progenitor cells1
    • Directed at CD34+ cells1,2
    • T cells induce apoptosis and haematopoietic failure1
  • Responsiveness of aplastic anaemia to immunosuppression has provided the strongest support for immune pathophysiology3
Pathophysiology of acquired aplastic anaemia: T-cell-mediated destruction of bone marrow3
  • Observations that supported an underlying immune pathophysiology:
    • Most patients show improvement after administration of antithymocyte globulins (ATGs), which causes transient T-cell depletion
    • Relapsed aplastic anaemia responds to repeated immunosuppression
      • For example restoration of blood counts to healthy levels after administration of low-dose ciclosporin (CsA)
    • The increasing proportion of responders as immunosuppressive regimens have intensified
      • From early attempts with corticosteroids to the use of high-dose cyclophosphamide (Cy)
  • Failure to respond to immunosuppression may indicate alternative pathophysiology, stem cell depletion, or immune mechanisms not susceptible to current therapies
