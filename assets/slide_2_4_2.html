Investigations required for the diagnosis of acquired aplastic anaemia
  • Bone marrow examination: marrow fragments should be readily obtainable from the aspirate
    • Difficulty obtaining fragments suggests a non-aplastic anaemia diagnosis
  • Bone marrow fragment features include:
    • Hypocellular with prominent fat spaces
    • Variable amounts of residual haematopoietic cells
    • Reduced or absent erythropoiesis
    • Reduced or absent megakaryocytes and granulocytes
    • Prominent lymphocytes, macrophages, plasma cells and mast cells
    • Possible lymphoid aggregates during the acute stage of the disease or when aplastic anaemia is associated with systemic autoimmune disease
    • No increase in reticulin
    • Presence or increased numbers of blasts is more consistent with hypocellular MDS or progression to leukaemia
  • Definition of aplastic anaemia severity is based on full blood count and bone marrow findings
