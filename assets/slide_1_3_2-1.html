Erythropoiesis
  • Feedback mechanism mediated by erythropoietin
  • Reduced numbers of circulating RBCs leads to lowered Hb
    • Lowered Hb leads to tissue hypoxia
  • Tissue hypoxia induces EPO synthesis in the kidneys
  • EPO binds to receptors on RBC progenitor cells (burst-forming units-erythroid and colony-forming units [CFUs]-erythroid)
    • Initiates a cascade of events leading to increased release of RBCs from the bone marrow
  • Increased numbers of Hb-carrying RBCs improves O2 delivery to tissues, after which EPO synthesis is reduced

Embryonic and fetal haematopoiesis
  • Human haematopoiesis begins outside the embryo (in the yolk sac) at ~3 weeks
    • Erythrocyte and granulocyte progenitors can be detected in the yolk sac at Day 25 of development
    • Yolk sac haematopoiesis ceases after 60 days
  • Onset of circulation allows yolk sac-derived blood cells to enter fetal tissues
    • Liver: first tissue to be colonised
      • Main blood-forming tissue at embryo stage before initiation of marrow haematopoiesis (at ~4.5–5 weeks)
  • Bone marrow haematopoiesis starts during Week 11 of development
    • Last tissue to develop during ontogenesis
